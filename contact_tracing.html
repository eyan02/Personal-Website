ContainIt

A group of fellow Duke students and I co-created a mobile app called ContainIt. It utilizes contact tracing to help mitigate the spread of COVID-19 using logitudinal location data to pin-point high-risk interactions with others infected with COVID-19.

Contact Tracing Solution

When a user self-identifies (and is verified) as being infected, they consent to share their location data to help back-trace their interaction(s) with other users.

Using contact-tracing algorithmns, we analyze potential contact events and provide users with notifications and actionable insight.

Contact Event(s)

Even minor interactions often don't meet safe social distancing requirements.

Example: the simulation below shows two people entering the same aisle in a supermarket. Person B (blue) tries to practice social distancing, but ends up near Person A (red) for a brief period of time.

Notice of Infection

Eleven days later, Person A (red) identifies as having been infected with COVID-19. A backtrace of their GPS location yields five contact events, including their contact with Person B (blue). Each contact event is analyzed to assess a number of user actionable insights.

Contact-Tracing Algorithmns

I developed contact-event algorithmns to isolate incident events from raw location data. The data is then processed to output actionable analytic metrics. The analysis enables users to learn from their contact events and receive real-time feedback and inform public health officials about COVID-19 transmission hotspots and the general publics practice of social distancing.

User Insights

The algorithmns transform user data into digestible information that protect users from serious contact events and enable real-time feedback about their proximity to other people.

For example, summary information about each contact event is displayed to the user, as well as a "social distancing grade": a letter grade that is calculated for each user based on their social distancing habits compared to CDC and WHO guidelines.
